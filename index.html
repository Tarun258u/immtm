IM : MTM

Initial Margin (IM) and Mark‑to‑Market (MTM). IM is satisfied primarily with Indian Government Securities (G‑Secs) which generates interest income in the form of coupon payments, MTM is typically held in cash.

IM — Indian Government Securities
MTM — Cash
Key point: IM can be provided as G‑Secs rather than cash while MTM remains in liquid cash to meet intraday variation needs.
Margin Structure
Scenario: Derivatives (Futures / Options)
Initial Margin (IM)
G‑Secs
IM is typically calculated to cover potential future exposure. Using Indian government bonds (G‑Secs) as margin allows FPIs to post securities that also yield coupons at a risk free rate.
Mark‑to‑Market (MTM / Variation Margin)
Cash
MTM covers day‑to‑day changes in market value and must remain highly liquid usually posted in cash.
FPIs provide:

G‑Secs + Cash

Benefit for FPI

Note: When posting G‑Secs as IM, Central Counter Parties (CCPs) usually apply a haircut to account for market risk. Hence margins provided against G-secs are slightly reduced

Income calculation (Example):
Suppose G‑Secs yield 6% coupon annually and a haircut of 3%. IM:MTM ratio is 80:20
If FPI wants to take a derivatives position of INR 100 crores
FPI will provide INR 103.09 (100/0.97) crore worth of G-Secs as IM and 20 crores of cash as MTM
FPI will earn an additional income of INR 6.2 crores as compared to providing entire IM:MTM in cash
